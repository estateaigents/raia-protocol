# Global Property Portal Market — Agent Fee Research
## Reference document for RAIA Protocol / MoveHome Foundation CIC
*Compiled: 20 April 2026 · Sources cited throughout*

---

## Executive Summary

Based on bottom-up aggregation of publicly reported revenues from listed portal companies, supplemented by estimated revenues from major unlisted portals, the global property portal industry generates approximately **USD 15–22 billion per year in total revenue**. The majority of this revenue comes directly from estate agent and developer subscription and advertising fees.

Listed companies alone account for approximately **USD 7.87 billion annually** (verified). The remaining USD 7–14 billion is spread across unlisted portals globally.

**There is no single verified third-party research figure for the precise global property portal TAM.** All total estimates in this document are reasoned estimates from public data, clearly labelled.

The **£1.5 billion class action filed at the UK Competition Appeal Tribunal in April 2026** is a matter of public record and is cited here as such.

---

## 1. Methodology

Bottom-up from listed company revenues (7 listed companies as anchor) plus extrapolation for unlisted markets using company filings, industry reporting from OnlineMarketplaces.com (formerly PropertyPortalWatch), and press coverage.

**Exchange rates (approximate, April 2026):** AUD/USD 0.65 · GBP/USD 1.33 · EUR/USD 1.08 · SEK/USD 0.095 · SGD/USD 0.74

**Fiscal year note:** REA Group and Domain Holdings use July–June fiscal year. All others use January–December.

## 4. Global TAM Estimate

**This is a reasoned estimate from public data — not a cited research figure.**

| Scenario | Global Portal Revenue |
|---|---|
| Low estimate | ~USD 10–12 billion |
| Central estimate | ~USD 15–18 billion |
| High estimate | ~USD 20–25 billion |

The US market (two major portals = ~USD 4.98bn) is by far the largest. Australia punches significantly above its weight (two portals = ~USD 1.35bn from 26 million people).

No verified third-party research report with a global property portal TAM figure was located. OnlineMarketplaces.com (formerly PropertyPortalWatch) does not publish a global TAM figure. This document represents the most comprehensive public aggregation of available data.

## 5. The Rightmove Class Action — Verified Citation

| | |
|---|---|
| Claim amount | £1.5 billion (GBP) / ~USD 2 billion |
| Date filed | 1 April 2026 |
| Filed by | Scott+Scott Attorneys at Law LLP |
| On behalf of | 250+ UK estate agents at filing; "thousands" described as represented |
| Forum | UK Competition Appeal Tribunal (CAT) |
| Nature of claim | Alleged abuse of dominant position; "unfair and excessive prices" in breach of Competition Act 1998 Chapter II and/or Article 102 TFEU |
| Share price impact | ~6% fall on filing date; ~£300m wiped from market cap (The Telegraph, 1 Apr 2026) |
| Status (20 Apr 2026) | Early-stage filing; no trial date set; Rightmove defending |
| Claimant website | https://www.portalfeescandal.co.uk |

**Key published quotes (public record):**

> "The case centres on allegations that [the portal] has abused its dominant position in the online property portal market by charging excessive and unfair prices." — One Essex Court, 1 April 2026

> "Estate agents have accused [the portal] of charging 'unsustainable' fees, with some saying their charges have more than doubled in recent years." — BBC, 1 April 2026

> "An estate agent who spoke to the BBC said they were charged £5,000 a month for a basic membership to advertise about 30–50 properties online." — The Observer, 4 April 2026

**Progression:**
- 13 November 2025: Scott+Scott pre-action announcement — "up to £1 billion"
- 1 April 2026: Formal claim filed at CAT — £1.5 billion
- 20 April 2026: Early proceedings; no trial date
